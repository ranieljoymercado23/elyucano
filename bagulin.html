ALABOK BURIAL CAVE (KEDLAP)

The Kedlap Burial Cave is a shallow cave overlooking the distant northern mountains where a natural ledge of stones serves as a resting place of the coffin and its accessories. Aside from the coffin, we also saw some offerings consisting of spears, a flute, a jar, deer antlers, an old outdated five peso bill, and even a bottle of Gin which denotes that all of which were placed recently and must have been jumbled with the old ones if there were any. The one that got your attention was the wood coffin with a cover shaped into a carabao (water buffalo). it has a rounded body, a head at one end and a tail at the other.
