AGOO TOURIST ATTRACTIONS


GIANT EAGLE OF THE NORTH PARK

The Giant Eagle of the North Park is a park and tourist attractions in Agoo, La Union. It features a congrete sculpture known as Eagle of the North by Anselmo Day-ag. The Eagle Sculpture is depicted with its wings spread as if it is preparing to take flight and symbolizes the influence of the Marcos Family.


MUSEO DE ILOKO

The Museo De Iloko, (also called the Agoo Museum or The Agoo Presidencia), is a heritage building and museum located in Agoo, La Union, known for its collection of "artifacts and other pieces of cultural importance to the Ilocanos", and for being one of few surviving examples of American Colonial Era architecture in the Ilocos Region.


BASILICA MINORE OF OUR LADY OF CHARITY (Basilica Menor de Nuestra Senora de la Caridad)

The Basilica Minore of our Lady of Charity, better known as Agoo Basilica, is a Roman Catholic minor basilica in Agoo, La Union, Phillipines dedicated to Our Lady of Charity. It is the sit of the Santa Monica Parish of the Roman Catholic Diocese of San Fernando de La Union under the Vicariate of St. Francis Xavier (Vicar Forane Raul S. Panay). It is situated along McArthur High Way in Agoo, La Union in front of the Jose D. Aspiras Civic Center.


ARINGAY TOURIST ATTRACTIONS


CENTENNIAL TUNNEL/ SALAPAC TUNNEL

Situated in Poblacion, Aringay, La Union, this 500 meter tunnel, 7.6 meter wide, and with a depth of 4.5 meters, was made in 1913 during the American Regime. Intended for the trains of the Philippine National Railways (PNR) bound for Baguio City, it was later abandoned by the Commonwealth Government because of the inpending Second World War. Similar structures could also be found in Tuba, Benguet, which where supposed to be part of the ambitious railway system.


PARADISO BEACH RESORT

Paradiso Beach Resort is situated in the hidden beaches of Samara, Aringay, La Union.


SAINT LUCY PARISH

Located in the town proper of Aringay, La Union, beside Notre Dame High School, Town Hall, Municipal Auditorium and Town Plaza. Prominent are the giant ageless historic Acacia trees surrounding the town proper.
